Observation 01

Your personal content gets 3-4x the engagement of your educational content - but it's only 20% of your feed.

Your top-performing original posts are personal: the LDR-to-marriage milestone, the business 5th birthday, the Vancouver photo dump. They pull 60-130+ likes. Your podcast education carousels - which make up around 30% of your feed - average closer to 15-25 likes. This is not a coincidence and it's not a problem with the education content itself. It's a blend issue. The posts that perform best are the ones where you - the researcher-turned-producer who burned a business down and rebuilt it - show up in the content. The show concept content works best when Cheryl is in the story, not just the thesis.

Data: avg likes on personal posts ~75 vs avg likes on pure educational carousel ~22. Top organic original post: LDR milestone at 267 likes.

Observation 02

You're producing award-winning audio every week and not turning any of it into Reels.

You have a 2x Asia Podcast Awards winning show - EDIT HISTORY - and you're now rebranding it to feature academics and researchers stress-testing their own theses. That is genuinely differentiated content. But your Reels average only 497 views, and you've posted just 16 of them across 100 posts. It's just not being activated on the platform where discovery happens.

Data: 71% carousels, 16% Reels. Reel avg views: 497 vs industry benchmark of 2,000-5,000+ for a niche creator. Podcast content not being cross-posted.

Observation 03

Sample Carousel - "The real reason your podcast isn't growing"

Slide 01

Most podcasters think they're not growing because their content isn't good enough. It's not that.

Slide 02

After years of working with podcasters - and building my own show from scratch - here's what I've actually observed: The shows that struggle aren't struggling because of quality. They're struggling because of concept.

Slide 03

Quality content gets you to episode 30. A differentiated show concept gets you to episode 300. Here's the difference: Quality = you can produce good episodes. Concept = there's a clear reason why someone would choose YOUR show over the 50 others in your niche.

Slide 04

When I sit down with a new client, I don't start with content calendars. I start with one question: What is this show uniquely positioned to do that most podcasts in this space aren't doing? When you can answer that with clarity... everything else - your topics, your guests, your marketing - starts to make sense. That's the leverage.

Slide 05

You're not behind because you haven't posted enough. You're behind because you haven't built the foundation yet. And foundations take time. That's intentional. Sounds good? Awesome. Let's get to work. 🎙️

I wrote this sample after reading 20+ of your captions in full.
